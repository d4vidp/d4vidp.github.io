Terms in the model

Cornea: Transparent outer covering of the eye that allows light to pass through
Imagine an ear of "corn" surrounding your eye.
Iris: Colored muscular structure that can expand or contract to control the passage of light into the eye.
Iris sounds like "Eye-rim"
Pupil: The pupil is actually an opening or hole in the eye that can expand or contract to let light through.
"The teacher's pupils light the way to the future"
Lens: Transparent structure behind the pupil that bends to focus light onto the retina
Remember this rhyme: "The lens bends and sends to the retina."
Retina: The thin inner tissue portion of the eye that contains millions of nerve cells, rods and cones, for transduction
"The retina has a plethora of rods and cones"
Rods: nerve cells in the retina that are used for peripheral vision and seeing black, white, or gray.
"Rods see broad and sod"
Cones: nerve cells in the retina that are used for fine details, color vision, and during the daylight.
"Cones see color up close when the clouds are out"
Fovea: an indentation in the eye where cones are concentrated. The best vision is obtained here.
"The FOVea has the best POV (Point of View)"
Blind Spot: location in the inner eye that contains no rods or cones, meaning that no transduction of visual stimuli occur here.
How to remember: When you are learning how to drive, you have to check your blind spot to make sure no cones or rods are there!
Optic Nerve: carries neural impulses from the visual stimuli to the brain.
"The optic nerve is exotic to the eye because it goes all the way to the brain"
